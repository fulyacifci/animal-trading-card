Honey badger

Honey badgers are not actually badgers at all! Their real name is Mellivora capensis, which translates to “honey eater of the cape”.

  • Habitat: Africa
  • Average lifespan: 24 years
  • Diet: Larvae and honey
  • Size: 55-77 cm

There is an amazingly special relationship between them and a small bird called a Honeyguide. The birds locate beehives and shout out and make the location known to the honey badgers, they then home in on where the indicative birds are and begin to break open the hive and feast on the honey.
